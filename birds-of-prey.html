BIRDS OF PREY

Red-Tailed Hawk

Color: Brown, white
Habitat: Grasslands
Food: Small Animals
Nesting: Trees
Behavior: Soaring
Fun Fact: Most common hawk in North America

Red-Shouldered Hawk

Color: Brown, white, reddish-brown
Habitat: Forests
Food: Mammals
Nesting: Trees
Behavior: Aerial Diving
Fun Fact: Return to the same nesting territory every year

Cooper's Hawk

Color: Gray, orange, white
Habitat: Forests
Food: Birds
Nesting: Trees
Behavior: Aerial Foraging
Fun Fact: Kill its prey by squeezing with its talons

Peregrine Falcon

Color: Dark gray, white
Habitat: Shorelines
Food: Birds
Nesting: Cliffs
Behavior: Aerial Diving
Fun Fact: Can reach speeds of 200mph while diving during a hunt

Turkey Vulture

Color: Light brown
Habitat: Open Woodlands
Food: Carcasses
Nesting: Cliffs
Behavior: Soaring
Fun Fact: Has a keen sense of smell allowing it to detect dead animals

Black Vulture

Color: Black
Habitat: Grasslands
Food: Carcasses
Nesting: Cliffs
Behavior: Soaring
Fun Fact: Extremely acidic stomach allows them to digest almost anything

Bald Eagle

Color: Brown, white
Habitat: Forests
Food: Fish
Nesting: Trees
Behavior: Soaring
Fun Fact: Steal prey from other birds

Osprey

Color: Brown, white
Habitat: Shorelines
Food: Fish
Nesting: Trees
Behavior: Aerial Diving
Fun Fact: Dive into water to catch prey
