INTRO TO BIRDING

Birding Supplies

  • Binoculars - the most essential tool to ensure a good view of birds flying high in the sky or perched on a tall tree
  • Camera - perfect for capturing memories and keeping track of your sightings, just be sure that the sounds and flash aren't disruptive to the wildlife!
  • eBird - an app that provides you with live information on nearby bird sightings
  • Merlin - an app that helps with species identification and logging your own sightings
  • Field Guide - a book that will help you identify birds and learn more about the species around you
  • Map/GPS - important for making sure you don't get lost in unfamiliar terrain

How To Start Birding

No matter how knowledgeable you might be about birds, the real fun comes from watching the behavior of these animals in their natural habitats. So, how do you actually get started with birdwatching? Well, the first step is finding a good location. You can explore local birding hotspots using community forums or apps such as eBird, which provide the latest information on where different species have been spotted. While some locations may be more fruitful than others, keep in mind that you can birdwatch nearly anywhere outdoors, it's simply a matter of keeping your eyes and ears open. If you're reluctant to immediately jump into an unfamiliar location, your backyard or a nearby park might be the perfect place to start. Pretty soon, you'll realize that birds are everywhere once you start looking for them.

Once you've chosen a location, the most important step is to simply start observing. Look for any movement in the bushes and trees around you, and don't forget to use your ears, because chances are that you'll hear a bird before you see it. With a little time and patience, you'll start noticing more birds than you ever expected. If you're not sure what you're seeing, New Jersey field guides or apps like Merlin can help you discover the different species in your area. A tip to keep in mind is that birds tend to be most active in the early morning, so the earlier you can get out, the better. And lastly, before you head out, be sure to reference our supply list to figure out what you might need to bring along!
